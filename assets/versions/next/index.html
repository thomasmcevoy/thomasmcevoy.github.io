THOMAS MCEVOY

ABOUT

Thomas McEvoy was born in Long Island, NY, and by age four was already teaching himself to play piano by ear. He soon began formal lessons, spending his early years studying the music of Beethoven, Bach, and Chopin. As a teenager, Thomas discovered jazz and dedicated himself to mastering this rich and expressive American art form. His earliest influences were pianists Ahmad Jamal and Thelonious Monk.

He accepted a scholarship to the University of North Florida where he studied with pianist Kevin Bales and legendary saxophonist Bunky Green. He also became a fixture on the Jacksonville jazz scene, learning lessons on the bandstand from veterans such as Von Barlow, Delbert Felix, Ben Tucker, Longineu Parsons, and Lawrence Buckner. In 2005 he won first place in the Philips Jazz Piano Competition. Notable performances from this period include a European tour with the UNF jazz band, North Sea Jazz Festival, Jazz à Vienne, Montreux Jazz Festival, East Coast Jazz Festival, Berks Jazz Festival, Savannah Jazz Festival, and Jacksonville Jazz Festival.

In 2006 Thomas moved to New York City, in search of further training and opportunity. He earned his masters degree from the Juilliard School, where he studied with Kenny Barron, one of the foremost living exponents of jazz piano. He toured internationally with Juilliard groups and performed or studied with such legends as Benny Golson, Gerald Wilson, Christian McBride, Wycliffe Gordon, Steve Turre, Kenny Washington, Ben Wolfe, and Rodney Jones.

Thomas has also been recognized as a promising composer. In 2010 he was selected to attend Betty Carter's Jazz Ahead at the Kennedy Center, where he refined his composing and performing skills under the tutelage of Dr. Billy Taylor, George Cables, Curtis Fuller, Winard Harper, and others.

Since graduation Thomas has performed at hundreds of venues, including Dizzy's Club Coca-Cola, Smalls Jazz Club, Iridium, Birdland, Smoke Jazz Club, and Zinc Bar. He has worked as a sideman with many of his generation’s most exciting artists, including Paul Sikivie, Lawrence Leathers, Alphonso Horne, Riley Mulherkar, Alex Nguyen, Randall Haywood, Robert Edwards, Vanessa Perea, Dave Baron, Joe McDonough, Theo Croker, Allan Mednard, Gilad Edelman, Chris Burbank, Angela Roberts, Karen Marie, and Andromeda Turre, to name a few. He also leads his own groups, which have included such stellar musicians as Grant Stewart, Phil Stewart, Ben Wolfe, Mike Karn, Neal Miner, David Wong, Paul West, Fukushi Tainaka, Aaron Kimmel, and Pete Van Nostrand.
